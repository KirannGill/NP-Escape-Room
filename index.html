Headaches Scenarios — Interactive Clinical Reasoning

Welcome to the NP Student Escape Challenge

Welcome to the NP Student Escape challenge. Today we will be delving into the complexities of headaches! In this experience, we will navigate through the intricate pathways of headache analysis, exploring the multitude of symptoms and causes, as well as evidence-based approaches to management. Get ready to test your knowledge against one of the most common ailments in the world.

Escape Room Learning Outcomes

Distinguish the pertinent positive clinical findings specific to primary and secondary headaches.

Using critical thinking, reasoning, and judgment, determine and prioritize the differential diagnosis.

Correctly identify red flag clinical findings for secondary headache disorders.

Establish and prioritize investigations or procedures specific to the suspected diagnosis.

Establish an evidence-based management plan specific to the identified headache diagnosis which includes pharmacological, non-pharmacological, patient education, and follow-up planning.

Activity Instructions

This virtual escape room is designed to strengthen your clinical reasoning and decision-making skills in the assessment and management of headache presentations.

What to Expect

You will work through three patient headache cases, each presenting a unique clinical scenario. Throughout the cases, you will:

Gather and interpret clinical information

Make diagnostic and management decisions at key clinical decision points

Complete interactive learning games and challenges

Apply evidence-informed approaches to headache assessment and treatment

Important Reminder

The primary goal of this activity is learning, not escaping. The games and challenges are designed to reinforce key concepts and support your clinical reasoning process.

Completion Certificates

Once you have worked through all three cases, download the corresponding certificate of completion for each escape room scenario. You will upload these to your Moodle course shell.

Successful completion: Download your Certificate of Completion.

Incomplete escape: Download your Certificate of Participation/No Completion.

Regardless of the outcome, engaging with the cases, reflecting on your clinical decisions, and engaging in the debrief exercise will help build your knowledge and clinical reasoning skills.

How Scoring Works

You earn 1 point for each correct answer across all three patient cases — this includes multiple-choice questions, true/false questions, drag-and-drop placements, and clinical decision points. A few free-response or reflection questions are practice-only and won't affect your score; these are clearly marked. You can see your running score at any time in the tracker at the bottom of the screen, and your final score and pass/fail result will be shown once you've completed all three cases.

Tips for Success

Read each case carefully.

Consider differential diagnoses for presentations to help refine your clinical reasoning skills using pertinent positive and negative findings from the patient history and presentation.

Think critically about your assessment findings and management options.

Learning is more important than the speed of completion.

Good luck, have fun, and enjoy the challenge of solving the headache cases!
